Projects

Outfit Recommender System (Final Thesis)

Developing an AI-based recommendation system designed to identify a user’s clothing style and suggest personalized outfits accordingly. The project focuses on understanding user preferences through data-driven representations rather than visual processing, leveraging modern recommender system techniques.

Instead of relying on computer vision, the system learns dense feature vector representations of clothing items from textual and categorical information such as product descriptions, colors, and style tags. These embeddings capture semantic relationships between items, allowing the model to recognize similarities in fashion aesthetics and trends.

Based on these learned representations, the model generates outfit recommendations that align with the user’s inferred style. The system combines content-based filtering and similarity search to assemble cohesive outfit suggestions, ensuring that items are stylistically consistent and relevant to individual preferences.

The project aims to explore how representation learning can enhance personalization in the fashion domain. By leveraging dense vector spaces and recommender algorithms, it provides an intelligent, scalable approach to helping users discover clothing that matches their unique tastes and identities.

Aigües de Barcelona Data Challenge

Our team participated as finalists in the Aigües de Barcelona Data Challenge, where we tackled the issue of increasing water demand caused by mass tourism in Barcelona, focusing particularly on its impact on the commercial sector. With over 8.2 million tourists visiting the city in 2023 compared to just 1.6 million residents, the project aimed to quantify and predict the effect of tourism on daily water consumption.

Using data provided by AGBAR, we developed a machine learning model to forecast water demand based on historical consumption, meteorological variables, population distribution, and tourism data. By integrating these sources, the model identified patterns linking climatic and touristic fluctuations to variations in water use, offering insights for more efficient and sustainable water management. The project also explored scenario simulations to anticipate future demand peaks, reinforcing Barcelona’s long-term resilience in managing critical urban resources.

Aina Hack
